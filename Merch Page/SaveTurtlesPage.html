How can YOU save turtles?

Here's How !

unsplash/image Reduce Ocean Plastic Waste

Plastic waste is one of the leading threats to marine life, including turtles. By reducing single-use plastics, properly disposing of waste, and participating in beach cleanups, we can minimize the risk of turtles ingesting or getting entangled in harmful debris. Every piece of plastic removed from the ocean is a step towards a safer habitat for turtles.

unsplash/image Protect Nesting Beaches

Turtles return to the same beaches to nest year after year, but many of these sites are threatened by human activities like construction and tourism. You can help by supporting conservation efforts that protect nesting grounds, keeping beaches clean, and avoiding disturbing turtles during nesting season. Each protected beach is a sanctuary for future generations of turtles.

unsplash/image Support Turtle-Friendly Fisheries

Thousands of turtles are accidentally caught in fishing gear each year. By supporting fisheries that use turtle-safe practices and advocating for regulations that protect marine wildlife, we can reduce this threat. Look for seafood products labeled as turtle-safe, and help push for global change in the fishing industry.

unsplash/image Prevent Water Pollution

Chemicals, oil spills, and other pollutants harm sea turtles and their habitats. Reduce chemical runoff by minimizing the use of harmful fertilizers and pesticides. Support efforts to prevent ocean dumping and raise awareness of the need for clean waters.

unsplash/image Educate and Advocate

Educate others about the importance of turtle conservation. Use social media, community events, and educational programs to raise awareness and promote actions that protect turtle populations.

Read More
unsplash/image Reduce Light Pollution

Artificial light can disorient hatchlings and prevent them from reaching the ocean. Support efforts to reduce light pollution on nesting beaches by using turtle-friendly lighting and advocating for improved regulations.

Read More
unsplash/image Participate in Beach Clean-Ups

Turtles often mistake litter for food, which can be fatal. Join or organize beach clean-up events to remove trash from turtle habitats and prevent it from ending up in the ocean.

KNOWN AS
GREEN SEA
TURTLE
Lifespan: 60-70 years
Found in: Tropical and subtropical waters around the world; often near coastlines, reefs, and islands.
Fun Fact: Unlike most sea turtles, adult green sea turtles are herbivores, primarily eating seagrass and algae, which gives their fat a greenish color.
KNOWN AS
HAWKSBILL SEA
TURTLE
Lifespan: 30-50 years
Found in: Coral reefs in tropical and subtropical oceans; mainly in the Atlantic and Indo-Pacific regions.
Fun Fact: Hawksbill turtles have a distinctive beak-like mouth that allows them to reach into crevices of coral reefs to feed on sponges and invertebrates.
KNOWN AS
LEATHERBACK SEA
TURTLE
Lifespan: 45-50 years
Found in: Open ocean; Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans. They are highly migratory and can be found from the Arctic to the Antarctic.
Fun Fact: Leatherbacks are the largest of all sea turtles and can dive deeper than any other turtle, reaching depths of over 1,200 meters (nearly 4,000 feet).
KNOWN AS
LOGGERHEAD SEA
TURTLE
Lifespan: 50+ years
Found in: Temperate and subtropical waters of the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans. Commonly found in coastal areas.
Fun Fact: Loggerheads have incredibly strong jaws that allow them to crush hard-shelled prey like crabs and mollusks.
KNOWN AS
OLIVE RIDLEY
SEA TURTLE
Lifespan: 50+ years
Found in: Warm waters of the Pacific and Indian Oceans, and the southern Atlantic Ocean.
Fun Fact: Olive Ridleys are known for their unique mass nesting behavior called "arribada," where thousands of females come ashore simultaneously to lay eggs.
